# The Chronicles of a Fragmented World 

Penned by [[Characters/Ainzbeth\|Ainzbeth]],  
In the 217th Year of the The Age of Accord


## On the first making 

The elves name it as the moment of creation 

Before time learned to wound itself, there was the Prime Creator. The elves know not where it came from, only that it held the power to create worlds and fill them with life. What they call the elder races.


From its hand came the shaping of everything we know. The elves remember this age not as myth but as our inheritance. The Gods walked the world and the world was whole.




## On the Common Era 

The age before everything changed. 

In those days, the elder races flourished, Elven cities sang with living magic. Dwarven halls carved eternity into stone. The world as we knew was not truly peaceful but it held a balance.


It must be recorded plainly: humanity did not yet exist, nor was it required. The planes were ordered, and the Gods had not yet bled.


“The technology and magic of this era might be lost to us forever, but my belief is that one day we may understand it, if we are allowed this privilege” - [[Characters/Ainzbeth\|Ainzbeth]] 

  

### The Day of Red Sun

At the end of the common era, the sky wailed as a red sun rose and bled into the sky, and with it came the full might of Hell in number beyond reckoning. Their assault was not merely on the world, but upon everything, even divinity itself.

On that day, The Prime Creator was slain, heaven itself fractured and war ceased to be distant or righteous it became survival itself. Reality cracked, and all that followed has been damage control. 

  
  

## [[The Mundane Plane/History/Age of Shattering/Age of Shattering\|Age of Shattering]]

What we humans call an age, the elves call an extinction event, measured in bodies.

For four millennia:

The mortal races and surviving gods fought the denizens of Hell and other outer plane entities who sided against us.

Entire elven bloodlines vanished between one season to the next, Dwarven forges fell silent one by one, demon hosts turned to ash, orc war clans died prematurely.

Their failing was not courage but time, the elder races do not breed quickly, they do not recover losses swiftly from annihilation. They were on the preface of total defeat itself. Faced with this slow erasure of their creations, the Gods made a choice born out of desperation. 


“Survival does not reward purity” - Commander Aelthyn Moonveil 

  

### On the Making of Humanity

Humanity was not discovered. It was designed.  
  
 “I did not completely agree with this plan, but it was necessary for the survival of life itself.” - Sorcerer Lokhir

Short lived, adaptable, numerous. A people who could be spent and replaced. 

When humanity was unleashed upon the war in full:
Battlelines held strong, lost territories were reclaimed, victory for the first time seemed attainable.

“We clung to ancient forms of war while the enemy evolved. Humanity adopted. We remembered” - Saeiron Tel’vas

Yet the cost was catastrophic.  
The Phaneton, divine servants, lesser gods and champions fell in droves, Even the might of Hell itself bled. The planes of fey burnt, and planes of shadow had light. Eventually the war started losing momentum, not because it was won but because there was no strength left to continue it at scale. The fires dimmed, but the hatred did not.

“I fought beside humans. They died screaming just as beautifully and meaningfully as we did” - Blade-Singer Rytael 


“Designed things are tools. Tools are discarded when they outlive their purpose.” - Archdruid Fenralaith 

“If we had been designed, perhaps we would still have cities” - Blade-Singer Vaelor

  

## The Age of Accord 

We humanity named this era, to mark it as our own.

By the war's end, the elder races stood at the edge of oblivion, and so the world passed quietly into human hands.

Human kingdoms rose where elven forest once stood. Human banners flew over the lands whose names they could not pronounce. Forgotten civilizations of old lay within land in ruin.

“Call it what it is: the Age of Inheritance, claimed by those arrived last” - Scholar Auran Klax

### On the fey and the judgment of other realms

The fey, and other beings see things through a different perspective. 

To them humanity is not a miracle:  
It is a weapon that outlived its purpose
A relic of divine failure 
A blight allowed to spread because none had the strength left to stop it

Their resentment and fear grows.  
“If this age truly is a dawn, then it rises over a graveyard beyond comprehension. May those who read this remember: the world was not saved, it was merely handed to the survivors.” - Fey correspondent 

  

### On the Surviving Gods 

[[Characters/Gods/Bahamut\|Bahamut]], he bound himself to humanity, acting as arbiter between rival kingdoms. 

Preventing their endless war from reigniting apocalypse. From the heavens he raised a great floating city, a watchful eye over the broken world, the humans call him a Protector, a living God. Others might say a Jailer. Bahamut stands ready to strike down any force mortal or otherwise that would plunge the world back into divine war.

“He stayed because he could not leave, A god who abandons his creation admits failure” - Corsair [[Characters/Merirosvo\|Merirosvo]]

We do not know if there are any other Gods who remain and side. But of those God we do know who are alive they wish us dead. They are still emboldened by their flame of ambition. To fulfill a purpose we do not understand.
