231st–232nd Year of the Age of Accord 

Following the devastation of the First Crusade, marked by the infamous [[The Mundane Plane/History/Age of Accord/The Battle of Ashen Night\|The Battle of Ashen Night]] in 217, [[Characters/Gods/Tiamat\|Tiamat]] rose once more. Though diminished, she had regained enough strength to rally her fractured armies and renew her campaign across [[The Mundane Plane/Vaeloris/The Northern Reach/The Northern Reach\|The Northern Reach]].

This second crusade lacked the overwhelming fury of the first; instead, it advanced as a grinding, persistent conflict. The Coalition, now more unified and militarised under its centralisation efforts, met her forces with coordinated resistance. Progress for Tiamat became slow and costly, each victory hard-won and each defeat a reminder that the world was no longer as unprepared as it had once been.

# Key Battles

- **The Breaking of the Ember Pass** (231): The opening strike. Tiamat's forces smashes through an unprepared Coalition defence, gaining early momentum and reopping a critical path into the Northern Reach
- **The Ashen Raids** (231): Scorched Earth attacks across[[ the Mantle\| the Mantle]] targeting settlements and farm land. These raids were to spread terror more than strategic gain, devasting civilian population and devastating the region.
- **The Stand of Hollow March** (232): The first major check on Tiamat's advance. A reinforced Coalition force held the line against repeated assaults, proving her armies could be resisted when properly organised. This Fort held for 14 months before it fell.
- **Operation Head-Taker** (232): Targeted strikes against Tiamat's commanders, supply chains and leadership structure. While her haze was set of Hollow March. 
- **Sudden Withdrawal** (Late 232): Without warning, Tiamat's campaign stopped, her armies retreated to what they controlled, leaving behind ruined lands but no decisive finial battle. The reasons remain unclear.
